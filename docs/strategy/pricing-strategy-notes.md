# Clairo Pricing Strategy Notes

**Date**: 2026-04-01
**Context**: AI SaaS pricing models are shifting from seat-based to outcome-based. Source: [AI SaaS Models That Actually Make Money in 2026](https://medium.com/the-ai-studio/ai-saas-models-that-actually-make-money-in-2026-af5eaf7bfd95)

---

## Industry Signal

Seat-based SaaS pricing is under pressure in 2026. When AI agents replace headcount, charging per-seat means fewer seats sold. SaaStr hit 140% YoY revenue growth (Q1 2025 → Q1 2026) with 1.25 human sales staff and 20+ AI agents — proof that outcome-based models work.

The winners are pricing on **the unit of value to the customer**, not on cost to serve or number of logins.

## Clairo's Value Units

For accounting practices, the measurable outcomes Clairo delivers are:

| Outcome | Metric | Current Manual Cost |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| BAS preparation time saved | 15-30 mins → 2 mins per client | ~$50-100/client in staff time |
| Tax planning data pull | Eliminates manual Xero → spreadsheet entry | ~$30-50/client |
| Tax code resolution | AI resolves unmapped transactions | Hours of manual classification |
| Compliance risk reduction | AI flags Part IVA, anti-avoidance, missing codes | Unquantifiable but high value |
| Advisory revenue enabled | Tax planning tool lets practice bill advisory fees | $500-2000+ per client engagement |

## Pricing Model Options

### Option A: Per-Client-Managed (Recommended starting point)

Charge per active Xero connection (i.e., per client the practice manages in Clairo).

- **Pros**: Directly tied to value. Scales with practice size. Simple to understand. A practice with 100 clients gets 10x the value of one with 10 clients and pays accordingly.
- **Cons**: Practices may hesitate to connect all clients upfront.
- **Example tiers**: $5-15/client/month depending on features enabled.

### Option B: Per-Deliverable (Outcome-based)

Charge per BAS lodged, per tax plan generated, per advisory report exported.

- **Pros**: True outcome pricing — they pay when they get a deliverable. Zero risk for the practice.
- **Cons**: Revenue is lumpy (seasonal for BAS/tax planning). Harder to forecast.
- **Example**: $20/BAS, $50/tax plan, $10/advisory report.

### Option C: Tiered by Client Count

Fixed monthly tiers based on number of clients managed.

- **Pros**: Predictable for both sides. Easy to communicate. Natural upgrade path.
- **Cons**: Less precise value alignment than per-client.
- **Example tiers**:
  - Starter: Up to 20 clients — $99/month
  - Professional: Up to 75 clients — $249/month
  - Growth: Up to 200 clients — $499/month
  - Enterprise: Unlimited — custom pricing

### Option D: Per-Seat (Weakest for AI SaaS)

Flat fee per accountant login.

- **Pros**: Industry-familiar model.
- **Cons**: A solo practitioner with 100 clients gets the same value as a 5-person firm with 100 clients but pays 1/5th the price. Misaligned incentives. This is the model the industry is moving away from.

### Option E: Hybrid — Base + Usage

Small base fee per practice + per-client or per-deliverable charges.

- **Pros**: Guaranteed minimum revenue + usage upside. Practice commits with skin in the game.
- **Cons**: More complex to communicate.
- **Example**: $49/month base + $5/active client/month.

---

## Key Principles

1. **Price on the client engagement, not the accountant login** — the unit of value is the client served, not the person doing the work.
2. **Align with how accountants already bill** — they charge clients per engagement (BAS lodgement, tax planning session), so per-deliverable pricing feels natural.
3. **Make the ROI obvious** — if a practice charges $300/BAS and Clairo costs $20/BAS, the 15x ROI sells itself.
4. **Start simple, evolve with data** — launch with tiered client count pricing, layer in outcome-based pricing once we have usage data from the EOFY trials.
